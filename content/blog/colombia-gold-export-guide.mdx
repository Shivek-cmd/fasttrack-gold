Colombia is one of the world's most significant gold-producing nations, with centuries of mining heritage and some of the richest mineral deposits on the continent. Yet exporting gold from Colombia requires navigating a detailed legal and regulatory framework designed to ensure traceability, prevent illegal mining, and protect the environment.

This guide walks you through everything you need to know.

## Why Colombia?

Colombia's gold production ranks among the top in Latin America. The country's diverse geography — from the Andes mountain ranges to the Pacific lowlands — creates ideal geological conditions for gold deposits. Major producing regions include Antioquia, Chocó, Bolívar, and Valle del Cauca.

Beyond volume, Colombian gold is prized for its purity and traceability — provided it comes through properly certified channels.

## The Legal Framework

The Colombian government has established a comprehensive framework for mineral exports through several key agencies and instruments:

**Agencia Nacional de Minería (ANM)** — The National Mining Agency oversees all mineral titles, production records, and legal mining operations in Colombia.

**DIAN (Dirección de Impuestos y Aduanas Nacionales)** — Colombia's tax and customs authority handles the actual export procedures, tariffs, and customs declarations.

**Ministerio de Minas y Energía** — Sets overall mining and energy policy including regulations governing precious mineral exports.

## Required Documentation

Every gold export through a certified commercializer requires the following documents:

### 1. Rucom Registration

The Registro Único de Comercializadores de Minerales (Rucom) is the essential first document. It registers your operation with the ANM and establishes the legal identity of your mining or trading operation. Without an active, current Rucom registration, no gold export can proceed.

### 2. Certificate of Origin

The Certificate of Origin establishes where the gold was mined — which specific title, mine, or region. This document is critical for international buyers who need assurance that the gold was extracted legally and ethically.

### 3. RUT (Registro Único Tributario)

The RUT is Colombia's unified tax registration document issued by DIAN. It functions as the business identifier for all tax and customs purposes. Your RUT must be fully up to date before any export process begins.

### 4. Mining Title Documentation

All suppliers must have valid, active mining titles or purchase contracts that trace the mineral back to its legal source. These are cross-verified against the ANM's official records.

## Customs and Logistics

Once compliance is cleared, the export logistics process begins:

1. **Customs Declaration** — Filed with DIAN, specifying mineral type, weight, purity, and destination country
2. **Physical Inspection** — Customs agents may conduct physical verification of the shipment
3. **Export License** — Issued upon successful clearance of all documentation
4. **International Shipping** — Coordinated through our certified transport agents with full insurance and tracking

## International Buyers: What to Look For

If you are an international buyer sourcing Colombian gold, always verify:

- That your supplier holds active Rucom registration
- That Certificates of Origin can be independently verified
- That the commercializer you work with has a formal compliance process
- That all export documentation is provided in full before payment
