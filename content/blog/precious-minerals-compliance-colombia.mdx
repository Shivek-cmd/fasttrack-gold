Colombia's precious minerals sector is one of the most tightly regulated in Latin America. This is a strength, not a weakness — robust regulation means that compliant operators can demonstrate clean provenance to the most demanding international buyers.

Understanding the compliance landscape is essential for any supplier or buyer engaging with Colombian gold or emeralds.

## The Regulatory Ecosystem

Three agencies form the core of Colombia's mineral regulation framework:

**Agencia Nacional de Minería (ANM)** registers and monitors all mining titles, maintains the Rucom database, and enforces mining law. Any mineral that moves through the formal export channel must have an ANM-verifiable origin.

**DIAN (Dirección de Impuestos y Aduanas Nacionales)** manages all customs and tax procedures. Every export declaration passes through DIAN, which cross-references documentation against the ANM's records.

**Ministerio de Minas y Energía** sets the policy framework and periodically updates the legal requirements for mining and export operations.

## What Is Rucom?

Rucom — the Registro Único de Comercializadores de Minerales — is the central registry for all entities that buy and sell minerals in Colombia. Registration is mandatory for any commercializer operating in the formal sector.

The Rucom database is publicly accessible, meaning that any buyer or compliance officer can verify a supplier's registration status in real time. At Fast Track Gold, this is the first verification we perform on every new supplier.

## Certificate of Origin: Tracing the Mineral

The Certificate of Origin is perhaps the most critical document in the Colombian mineral export chain. It establishes:

- The specific mine or mining title where the mineral was extracted
- The extraction date and volume
- The chain of custody from mine to commercializer

International buyers — particularly those operating under due diligence requirements in the EU, UK, or US — rely on the Certificate of Origin to satisfy their own compliance obligations. A properly issued Colombian Certificate of Origin is a powerful document.

## The RUT and Tax Compliance

The RUT (Registro Único Tributario) is Colombia's universal tax registration system administered by DIAN. All entities operating in the formal economy must have a valid, current RUT. For mineral exporters, the RUT is required for all customs declarations and must be kept up to date with accurate information about the registered entity.

## For International Buyers: Your Due Diligence

If you are purchasing Colombian precious minerals on the international market, your own compliance obligations likely require you to verify your supplier's compliance. Questions to ask:

- Is the commercializer registered in Rucom? (Verify on the ANM platform)
- Can they provide a valid Certificate of Origin for each shipment?
- Is their RUT current and correctly reflecting their registered entity?
- What is their compliance process for supplier onboarding?
