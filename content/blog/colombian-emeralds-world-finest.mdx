When gemologists and jewellers speak of the world's finest emeralds, they inevitably point to one origin: Colombia. With a history stretching back to pre-Columbian civilizations, Colombian emeralds have commanded premium prices and captivated buyers across every continent for centuries.

## What Makes an Emerald "Colombian"?

An emerald is a variety of the mineral beryl (Be₃Al₂Si₆O₁₈), coloured green by trace amounts of chromium and sometimes vanadium. While emeralds are found in many countries, Colombian emeralds are distinguished by their exceptional colour saturation and a warm, slightly yellowish-green tone that gemologists refer to as "Colombian green."

The unique geological conditions in Colombia's three main emerald-producing regions — Muzo, Chivor, and Coscuez in the Eastern Andes — produce stones with a crystalline clarity and depth of colour that is virtually impossible to replicate elsewhere.

## The Three Pillars of Colombian Emerald Quality

### Colour

Colour is the single most important factor in emerald valuation. Colombian emeralds are prized for their vivid, saturated green — neither too blue nor too yellow — with a brightness that holds under different lighting conditions. The finest stones exhibit what the trade calls "garden" — an even distribution of colour with minimal zoning.

### Clarity

Unlike diamonds, emeralds are almost universally included — the presence of internal features (called *jardin*, French for "garden") is expected and even accepted. However, Colombian emeralds, particularly those from the Muzo region, frequently exhibit exceptional transparency with inclusions that do not detract from the stone's beauty.

### Provenance

For today's ethical jewellery market, provenance matters as much as the stone itself. Colombian emeralds sourced through legal, certified channels carry documentation that traces them from mine to market — ensuring buyers that their stone was not associated with illegal mining or conflict.

## Colombia's Dominance in Global Supply

Colombia accounts for an estimated 70–90% of the world's premium emerald supply. The Muzo region alone has been mined continuously for over 400 years. Despite this long history of extraction, Colombia's geological reserves continue to produce exceptional stones — a testament to the richness of the country's mineral endowment.

## Legal Sourcing: The Non-Negotiable

The emerald trade has historically been associated with informal mining and opacity in the supply chain. Colombia has taken significant steps to formalise and regulate the sector, requiring:

- **Mining title documentation** for all producing operations
- **Certificate of Origin** for all export shipments
- **Rucom registration** for all commercializers
- **Customs declaration** through DIAN for all international shipments
